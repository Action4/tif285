Lab sessions

The computer lab sessions will be livestreamed via zoom with the possibility for remote supervision. The Thursday morning sessions will typically start with a demonstration or feedback discussion led by one of the teaching assistants (see the ).

During the lab sessions you are expected to work on exercises, problem sets and projects and you will have the opportunity to discuss with the supervisors and with your fellow students. Concerning which computer to use, you have three options:

  • Use one of the computers in rooms F-T7203, F-T7204, FT4011. These rooms are reserved for us on Thursdays from 8-12. They can also be used other times if available.
  • Log in remotely to one of the Chalmers STUdat linux computers. See instructions at https://chalmers.topdesk.net/. Depending on your platform, find the relevant set of instructions for "How do I remotely access a StuDAT Linux computer from my [select platform]". You might want to use the /bin/bash shell rather than the default /bin/sh.
  • Use your personal computer.
In all cases you need to have your own headset (microphone and headphones) for remote interactions, and you first need to follow the instructions.

We will be using zoom breakout rooms for remote group work and supervision. Please follow the instructions from the teaching assistants after joining the lab sessions.

Yata discussions

We will be using the learning platform to facilitate group discussions between students and with teachers. Feel free to post questions here, and to contribute with answers and suggestions. The platform is inspired by the Stack Exchange forums that are widely used in, e.g., coding communities. You can up- or down-vote posts and you can mark accepted answers. Our aim is to encourage collective efforts and increase individual learning. The interface allows various advanced formatting: (i) latex for math expressions, (ii) code blocks, (iii) links, and you can upload images. Posts can be made anonymously (except for teachers whose names are always displayed).
